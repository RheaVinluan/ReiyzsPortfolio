Birthday Blues

Published on: The QCU Times Publication

The candles flickered, casting golden shadows across the room. Their tiny flames stood proudly on the blue birthday cake, while voices around me rose in cheerful chorus. It should have felt like a feast, a celebration. A moment to be grateful.

Yet my heart refused to join.

Another year. Another flame snuffed out with a single breath. Another 365 days gone, carrying with them questions that never found their answers. Where am I going? What do I truly want? Each birthday, the same question lingers like a haunting refrain: What’s your wish?

And still, I have none.

I smiled—because that’s what was expected—though my lips carried no truth. When I closed my eyes and leaned toward the candle, my mind was an empty sky. No wishes, no visions, no stars to guide me. The flame vanished, and with it, a little of mine did too.

Year after year, as the candles dim, I feel as though I am dimming with them. Each spark lost is another piece of me fading into shadow. Once, I burned brightly—proud, alive, full of laughter. Now, I barely glow.

Blue. A color so beautiful in paint and ocean waves, yet so heavy when it settles in the soul. It clings, cold and endless, even in a room filled with warmth.

Surrounded by laughter, I still feel the tide rising within me—soft at first, then crashing—until it drowns out all sound but the echo of my own silence.

Why must the color blue arrive on the day meant to be golden? Why must sorrow wear the crown of celebration?

I want to shine—radiant as the noonday sun, unyielding as its light. I want to sparkle like the stars that pierce the velvet sky. Yet instead, the waves of blue break against me, and I am left with salt on my cheeks, the kind no sea could ever wash away.

Even in the midst of a crowd, I find myself lying alone on the imagined shoreline, staring at a sky too dark to dream beneath. I try, year after year, to conjure a wish—something to hold, something to chase. But nothing comes.

Perhaps these tears are not weakness, but seeds. Perhaps this ache is the quiet labor of becoming.

And so, tonight, though my mind was empty before the flame, I let a wish take root at last. Not whispered into the air, not carved into silence, but planted in the secret soil of my heart.

Next year, may the cake be yellow.
