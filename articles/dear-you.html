Dear You,

Published on: The QCU Times Publication

How are you today? I hope this letter finds you well, carried gently by the invisible hands of time, until it finally rests in your grasp. There is something magical in receiving a letter—like a bird alighting on your windowsill with a message from far away. It stirs the heart, doesn’t it? Suddenly, the ordinary moment is dressed in joy, and you feel remembered, cherished, and seen. On days that feel heavy, when the world seems to pile stones on our shoulders, a letter becomes a soft lantern—lifting the darkness, even for a little while.

Perhaps that is the beauty of this day, a day dedicated to writing letters. To remind us that we are never too distant, never too late, never too lost to send a piece of ourselves across paper and ink. Have you ever thought of writing one, too? To a friend, a loved one, or even to yourself? Words, when given freely, bloom into gifts—letters that whisper love, gratitude, and care.

Words are powerful, as mighty as swords on a battlefield. Yet unlike swords, which wound and divide, letters mend and weave. A single sentence, written with tenderness, can be stronger than any armor, braver than any soldier. Your words, whenever they find me, carry warmth into my gray days, as if you were standing beside me, fighting against despair with nothing but your heart. Every letter from you feels like a golden sunrise breaking through my night.

As I write this, I realize that each stroke of ink is a small bridge between us. Every curve, every dot, every sentence feels like a thread of an invisible tapestry, binding our hearts across distance. Letters, whether handwritten or typed, seem alive—breathing with longing, carrying laughter, holding silence. And within them, there is always a promise, you are not alone. Even in the absence of touch, there is presence. Even in silence, there is comfort.

So let us keep writing. Let us fill the world with letters that smell of ink, taste of memory, and sound like love. For each word we send becomes a lantern in the dark, a song in the stillness, a hand reaching out across miles.

And when you open this one, I hope you feel me there—alive in every line, smiling between the paragraphs, waiting for the moment your heart answers back.

Always,
Me
