But What Comes After College?

An Open Diary From a Girl at the Edge of Herself

The sun rises in the east, faithful and indifferent. It climbs the sky, then slowly disappears into the horizon — again, and again, and again. Time passes in repetition, in routine, like counting eggs on a tray each morning, careful not to crack anything fragile. Calendars lose their meaning when each square feels the same. The light slips through my curtains, soft and golden, yet it strikes my face like a memory I didn’t ask for.

I woke up.

Not because I want to — but because the world insists. My eyes open to the ceiling I’ve memorized too well.

What should I do today?

The question stings like cold water. It’s always there. Lingering. Heavy.

Inside me lives a girl—blurred and shrinking—trapped in a world she doesn’t understand. A girl who used to have answers. A girl who used to dream.

Life is a book of riddles, with pages torn and meanings lost in translation. Questions throb in my mind like bruises I can’t hide: Where am I going? Will I be okay? Will I be happy? The silence doesn't reply.

I move through the morning as if underwater. My limbs feel borrowed. I boil water for coffee like a ritual that never resurrects me. I sip slowly, tasting more fear than caffeine. And in the quiet steam rising from my mug, the thought returns—is there really a future for me?

It loops in my mind like a song I never liked but can’t stop humming. My soul aches like it’s been sprinting in circles. I’m exhausted—and I haven't even begun.

The walk to the jeepney station feels automatic now. My feet know the way better than I do. I've spent most of my life chasing medals, applause, scores printed on paper—proof that I was doing something right. Honors, quiz bees, contests... I chased them all, thinking they’d lead somewhere. And yet, now that the finish line nears, I realize no one told me what comes after.

I sit in class, pretending to listen. My body is present. My mind is elsewhere—floating in a fog. I win at academics. I meet expectations. But what comes after?

Three hours. Half of it was spent wrestling questions I can’t answer. My future stretches ahead like a hallway with no lights. I walk it blind, hands outstretched, praying not to fall—but maybe hoping I do.

Evening comes, and I return to my room. My sanctuary, my cell. The clutter mirrors my mind. Echoes of yesterday’s cries hum in the corners. I let my knees give way to the weight of it all. I sink to the floor as tears fall—suddenly, silently. I don’t know what broke me this time. Maybe nothing did. Maybe everything did.

They say I should be proud. That I have potential. That I’ll succeed.

They say.

But they don’t see me like this—knees to the floor, palms to the sky, whispering to God: If You love me, take me now.

Because this life? It feels like punishment. A cruel game for a girl who just wanted to understand herself. A girl who walked into a room called Adulthood, only to find the door locked behind her.

I try to picture my future. But the images won’t come. My dreams used to dance like stars — bright, infinite, reachable. Now they’re gone. Or worse, they’ve abandoned me, too.

Isn’t it funny? In high school, I had plans. Blueprints. Vision boards. I was a girl with fire in her chest and the world at her feet. And now? I'm barely a spark. A breath. A question mark.

Where did she go?
Where did I go?

The night grows deeper. The silence is louder. The questions—still running. Still racing.

And I? I am tired.

So I ask again. To the ceiling. To the wind. To God.

What comes after college?
